Reversal Chart Patterns

Head and Shoulders Top Pattern Analysis

The Head and Shoulders Top is a bearish reversal pattern that occurs after an uptrend, signaling a potential reversal to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Left Shoulder: A peak followed by a decline in price.
  • Head: A higher peak formed after the left shoulder, followed by a price decline.
  • Right Shoulder: A peak that is similar in height to the left shoulder, followed by a decline.
  • Neckline: A line connecting the lows formed between the shoulders, indicating the breakout level.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after an uptrend, suggesting a reversal to a downtrend.
  • Confirms when the price breaks below the neckline.
  • Indicates that bulls are losing strength, and sellers are gaining control.
  • Targets the price move equivalent to the distance between the head and neckline.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When the price breaks below the neckline.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the right shoulder or the head (depending on the strategy).
  • Take Profit: Based on a target from the neckline to the top of the head or at significant support levels.

Head and Shoulders Bottom Pattern Analysis

The Head and Shoulders Bottom (also known as the Inverse Head and Shoulders) is a bullish reversal pattern that forms after a downtrend and signals a potential reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Left Shoulder: A price decline followed by a short-term rally.
  • Head: A deeper decline creating a lower low, followed by another rally.
  • Right Shoulder: A decline that doesn’t go as low as the head, followed by an upward move.
  • Neckline: A resistance level connecting the highs after the left shoulder and the head, which is broken on confirmation.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a downtrend.
  • Indicates a possible bullish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation is needed when price breaks above the neckline.
  • Signals that bears may be losing control and buyers are gaining strength.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks and closes above the neckline.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the right shoulder low.
  • Take Profit: Measured by the distance from the head to the neckline, projected upward from the breakout point.

Double Top Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Double Top is a bearish reversal chart pattern that forms after an extended uptrend and indicates a potential shift to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Two peaks: The price reaches a high point twice, with a moderate decline in between.
  • Resistance level: Both peaks occur near the same price level, forming strong resistance.
  • Neckline: The low point between the two tops acts as the neckline; a break below it confirms the pattern.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after an uptrend.
  • Signals a potential bearish reversal.
  • Confirmation is given when price breaks below the neckline with volume.
  • Indicates that buying momentum is weakening and selling pressure is increasing.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks and closes below the neckline.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the highs of the double top.
  • Take Profit: Projected by measuring the height from the top to the neckline and extending downward.

Double Bottom Pattern Analysis

The Double Bottom is a bullish reversal pattern that forms after a downtrend and signals a potential reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Two distinct lows: Roughly at the same price level, indicating strong support.
  • Intervening peak: A peak between the two lows that forms resistance.
  • Breakout point: When price breaks above the intervening peak, confirming the pattern.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a downtrend.
  • Indicates a possible bullish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation occurs when price breaks above the resistance level formed by the peak.
  • Shows that sellers failed to push the price lower twice, and buyers are gaining strength.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks and closes above the resistance (neckline).
  • Stop-Loss: Below the recent low (second bottom).
  • Take Profit: Based on the height of the pattern projected upwards from the breakout point.

Triple Top Chart Pattern Analysis

The Triple Top is a bearish reversal pattern that forms after an extended uptrend and signals a potential trend reversal to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Three peaks: Price reaches a similar high three times, with pullbacks in between.
  • Horizontal resistance: Each peak touches the same resistance level.
  • Neckline support: A horizontal support level is formed from the pullbacks between the peaks.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after an uptrend.
  • Signals a potential bearish reversal.
  • Volume often decreases with each peak.
  • Breakdown below the neckline confirms the pattern.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks below the neckline with strong volume.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the last peak of the triple top.
  • Take Profit: Measured by the height of the pattern projected downward from the neckline.

Triple Bottom Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Triple Bottom is a bullish reversal pattern that forms after a downtrend and signals a potential reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Three distinct lows: All occurring near the same price level, showing strong support.
  • Equal or nearly equal troughs: Indicating repeated testing of support without breaking lower.
  • Volume increase: Typically increases near the third bottom and during the breakout.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a downtrend.
  • Indicates a possible bullish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation is needed with a breakout above the resistance level formed by the highs between the bottoms.
  • Signals that bears may be losing control and buyers are starting to gain momentum.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks above the resistance level formed by the highs between the bottoms.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the third bottom’s low.
  • Take Profit: Based on the height of the pattern projected upward from the breakout point or at key resistance levels.
