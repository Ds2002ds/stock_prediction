Hammer Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Hammer is a bullish reversal candlestick pattern that appears after a downtrend, indicating a potential trend reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Small real body: The body is small and located near the top of the candle.
  • Long lower shadow: At least twice the length of the real body, indicating rejection of lower prices.
  • No or very small upper shadow: Signaling strong buying pressure.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after a downtrend.
  • Indicates potential bullish reversal.
  • Confirmation is needed with a follow-up bullish candle.
  • Used by traders to identify buying opportunities.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price moves above the high of the hammer candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the low of the hammer candle.
  • Take Profit: At key resistance levels or using a risk-reward ratio.

Doji Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Doji is a neutral or indecision candlestick pattern that indicates a potential reversal or pause in the current trend, depending on the context in which it appears.

Pattern Composition:

  • Very small or no real body: The open and close prices are virtually equal.
  • Upper and lower shadows: Can be present and vary in length.
  • Cross-like appearance: Often resembles a plus sign or a cross.

Key Points:

  • Indicates indecision in the market.
  • Can signal reversals or continuation depending on the trend.
  • More significant when occurring after a strong trend.
  • Confirmation is needed from the next candlestick.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: Based on confirmation from the following candle direction.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the low (for bullish) or above the high (for bearish) of the doji candle.
  • Take Profit: At key support/resistance or based on price action.

Inverted Hammer Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Inverted Hammer is a bullish reversal pattern that forms after a downtrend and signals a potential reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Small real body: The body is near the bottom of the candle.
  • Long upper shadow: At least twice the length of the body, indicating rejection of higher prices.
  • Little or no lower shadow: Shows that sellers were not in full control.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a downtrend.
  • Indicates a possible bullish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation with a strong bullish candle is essential.
  • Signals that bulls are starting to gain control.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: On a break above the high of the inverted hammer candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the low of the inverted hammer.
  • Take Profit: At nearby resistance levels or based on a favorable risk-reward ratio.

Dragonfly Doji Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Dragonfly Doji is a bullish reversal candlestick pattern that often appears at the bottom of a downtrend, signaling potential market reversal to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Doji body: The open and close are at or near the same level, forming a small or nonexistent body.
  • Long lower shadow: Indicates strong buying interest after sellers pushed prices lower.
  • No upper shadow: Shows buyers regained control before the close.

Key Points:

  • Appears at the bottom of a downtrend.
  • Suggests a possible bullish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation with a strong bullish candle is important for validity.
  • Signifies market indecision followed by buying strength.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: On a breakout above the high of the Dragonfly Doji candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the low of the doji shadow.
  • Take Profit: At nearby resistance levels or based on a predefined risk-reward strategy.

Shooting Star Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Shooting Star is a bearish reversal pattern that forms after an uptrend and signals a potential reversal to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Small real body: The body is near the lower end of the candle.
  • Long upper shadow: At least twice the size of the body, showing strong rejection of higher prices.
  • Little or no lower shadow: Indicates weak buying pressure at the bottom.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after an uptrend.
  • Suggests a possible bearish reversal.
  • Confirmation is required with a bearish candle following the pattern.
  • Shows that buyers tried to push the price up but failed.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price drops below the low of the shooting star candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the high of the shooting star.
  • Take Profit: At nearby support levels or using a risk-reward ratio strategy.

Hanging Man Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Hanging Man is a bearish reversal candlestick pattern that typically appears after an uptrend and signals a potential trend reversal to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Small real body: Positioned near the top of the candle.
  • Long lower shadow: At least twice the size of the real body, suggesting selling pressure during the day.
  • Little or no upper shadow: Indicates weak buying pressure.

Key Points:

  • Occurs after an uptrend.
  • Indicates a potential bearish reversal.
  • Confirmation is required with a bearish candle closing below the hanging man.
  • Warns traders that bulls may be losing control.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When the price breaks below the low of the hanging man candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Just above the high of the hanging man.
  • Take Profit: At key support levels or based on a risk-reward setup.

Gravestone Doji Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Gravestone Doji is a bearish reversal pattern that forms after an uptrend and signals a potential reversal to the downside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Small real body: The body is located near the bottom of the candlestick.
  • Long upper shadow: At least two times the length of the body, indicating rejection of higher prices.
  • Little or no lower shadow: Suggests that sellers were in control during the session.

Key Points:

  • Appears after an uptrend.
  • Indicates a possible bearish trend reversal.
  • Confirmation is needed with a strong bearish candle.
  • Signals that bulls may be losing control and sellers are starting to dominate.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price moves below the low of the gravestone doji candle.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the high of the gravestone doji.
  • Take Profit: At key support levels or based on a favorable risk-reward ratio.
