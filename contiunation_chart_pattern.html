Continuation Chart Patterns

Symmetrical Triangle Chart Pattern Analysis

The Symmetrical Triangle is a continuation pattern that forms during a trend and typically signals a period of consolidation before the price continues in the direction of the prevailing trend.

Pattern Composition:

  • Converging trendlines: Formed by connecting lower highs and higher lows.
  • Volume contraction: Volume generally decreases as the pattern progresses.
  • Breakout point: A breakout occurs when the price moves outside the triangle, often accompanied by increased volume.

Key Points:

  • Can appear in both uptrends and downtrends.
  • Usually a continuation pattern, but can also signal a reversal in some cases.
  • Breakout direction is typically in line with the existing trend.
  • Confirmation comes from a breakout with strong volume.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks out above resistance or below support trendline with volume.
  • Stop-Loss: Just outside the opposite side of the triangle.
  • Take Profit: Measured move technique – the height of the triangle added/subtracted from the breakout point.

Ascending Triangle Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Ascending Triangle is a bullish continuation pattern that typically forms during an uptrend and signals a potential breakout to the upside.

Pattern Composition:

  • Flat resistance line: A horizontal line formed by connecting highs at a similar price level.
  • Rising trendline: A diagonal support line formed by connecting higher lows.
  • Converging price action: Price moves within the triangle, getting squeezed toward the apex.

Key Points:

  • Forms during an uptrend.
  • Suggests a potential bullish breakout once resistance is broken.
  • Volume often declines during the formation and increases on breakout.
  • Indicates buyers are gradually gaining strength.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks and closes above the resistance line.
  • Stop-Loss: Just below the rising trendline or recent swing low.
  • Take Profit: Measured by projecting the height of the triangle from the breakout point.

Descending Triangle Pattern Analysis

The Descending Triangle is a bearish continuation pattern that forms during a downtrend, indicating the potential for further downward movement.

Pattern Composition:

  • Flat support line: Price finds support at a consistent horizontal level.
  • Descending resistance line: Lower highs are formed, showing weakening buying pressure.
  • Volume often decreases: As the pattern forms, suggesting a breakout is near.

Key Points:

  • Typically forms during a downtrend.
  • Breakout usually occurs to the downside.
  • Confirmation is needed with a strong bearish breakout candle.
  • Indicates that sellers are gaining control as buyers fail to push prices higher.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks and closes below the support line.
  • Stop-Loss: Above the most recent lower high within the triangle.
  • Take Profit: By measuring the height of the triangle and projecting it downward from the breakout point.

Flag Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Flag is a continuation pattern that forms after a strong price movement and signals the potential resumption of the prevailing trend.

Pattern Composition:

  • Flagpole: A sharp price move (either upward or downward) that sets the trend direction.
  • Consolidation area (Flag): A small rectangular-shaped pullback or sideways movement against the main trend.
  • Breakout: Occurs when the price breaks out from the flag in the direction of the prior trend.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a strong trend, either bullish or bearish.
  • Indicates a potential trend continuation.
  • Volume typically contracts during the flag formation and expands at breakout.
  • Reliable in both uptrends (bullish flag) and downtrends (bearish flag).

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks out of the flag in the direction of the prior trend.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the flag in a bullish setup, or above the flag in a bearish setup.
  • Take Profit: Target usually equal to the length of the flagpole projected from the breakout point.

Pennant Candlestick Pattern Analysis

The Pennant is a continuation pattern that typically appears after a strong price movement (either up or down) and indicates that the prevailing trend is likely to resume.

Pattern Composition:

  • Strong flagpole: A sharp and nearly vertical price move, forming the "pole" of the pennant.
  • Converging trendlines: A small symmetrical triangle or pennant shape that forms after the flagpole, showing consolidation.
  • Breakout: Price typically breaks out in the direction of the prior trend.

Key Points:

  • Appears after a strong trending move.
  • Indicates a potential continuation of the trend.
  • Volume often decreases during the pennant formation and increases on breakout.
  • Reflects a pause in the market before the trend resumes.

Trading Strategy:

  • Entry: When price breaks out of the pennant in the direction of the prior trend.
  • Stop-Loss: Below the lower trendline for bullish pennants or above the upper trendline for bearish pennants.
  • Take Profit: Measure the flagpole height and project it from the breakout point.
